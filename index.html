What is Head Start?

Head Start is a free child care and pre-school program for kids from birth to 5 years old. We also provide services for the whole family, even pregnant mothers can enroll. Our program is for families who meet certain requirements.

Head Start has well-trained teachers who are devoted to the success of your whole family. Some locations offer full-day programs and others offer half-day programs.

Kids get to:

  • learn basic math and reading skills
  • interact with other kids their age
  • learn and have fun at the same time
  • eat healthy breakfast, lunch, and snacks
  • access free health services

Parents and guardians get to:

  • access free career training and job opportunities
  • meet other families with children
  • learn about child development
  • gain leadership skills

Are you pregnant?

We offer free services to help you and your new baby get ahead through the best health and education options.

Who is it for?

Head Start is for families with kids from birth to age 5. We provide custom learning plans for your child. We offer infant and toddler care, half-day programs, full-day programs, and home-based services.

You must meet certain guidelines to get into Head Start.

The first step is to look at how many people are in your home and how much money everyone earns.

How many people live in your home? Include everyone, and don’t forget to count the kids!

If you fit into the income guidelines above, you may qualify for Head Start. If you earn more money than what is in the chart, you may still be able to qualify because there are sometimes spots open for families with higher incomes. Call the centers near you to check.

There are other things that may help you qualify for the program. (You may need to provide proof.)

If you:

  • Are a foster parent, a single parent, or a teen parent.
  • Are unemployed.
  • Care for a child that is not yours.
  • Had a recent death in the household.
  • Had a spouse recently deported.
  • Were in jail or have a spouse in jail.
  • Have not graduated from high school or college.
  • Are a parent enrolled in an education or training program.
  • Are homeless.
  • Receive disability, TANF, SSI, or WIC benefits.
  • Have a disabled child, or have a referral to a pre-school program.
  • Have a child with chronic health problems or special needs.
  • Live in a house where there is substance abuse, physical abuse, or mental illness.
  • Do not have access to a car.
  • Speak English as your second language, are a new migrant family, or have seasonal work.
  • Are in the military.

Let our staff know if any of these apply to you. It may help you qualify.

Where is it located?

There are over 60 Head Start centers in Detroit, and there’s one near you! If you can’t make it to a center, there are also home programs in which a well-trained teacher visits your home once a week.

Use our tool to find a center new you.

How do I apply?

When you find a center near you, call and let us know that you want to apply. Our staff will tell you what to bring, and may schedule an appointment to meet you.

Our staff will help you apply when you come in. Plan on spending about an hour at this appointment. (You may bring children.) Share as much information as you can. We are looking for families who benefit most from our services.

YYou will need to bring certain papers to apply. Bring these things with you to make the process faster:

  • your child's birth certificate
  • your child's immunization records
  • if you work, proof of your income
  • if you attend school, proof of your student status
  • your driver's license or ID
  • your child's medical insurance information, if available
